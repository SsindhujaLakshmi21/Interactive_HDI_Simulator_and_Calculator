HDI Methodology

$$\text{HDI} = \sqrt[3]{\text{Health} \times \text{Education} \times \text{Income}}$$

The HDI is a geometric mean of three indices. Geometric mean shows that high income cannot compensate for low health or poor schooling.

Health Dimension normalization

Formula: (Life Expectancy - 20) / (85 - 20). Minimium value is set to 20 years, maximum to 85 years.

Education Dimension normalization

Formula: (MYS_Index + EYS_Index) / 2. MYS_Index = MYS / 15 and EYS_Index = EYS / 18.

Income Dimension normalization

Formula: (ln(GNI) - ln(100)) / (ln(75000) - ln(100)). Standardizes GNI per capita on a logarithmic scale.

Inequality-Adjusted HDI (IHDI)

Calculated as I_Dimension = Dimension_Index * (1 - Inequality_Discount), and IHDI is the geometric mean of these inequality-adjusted indices.
